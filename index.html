About me

I am a PhD researcher at the Berlin School of Economics and Law (HWR Berlin), specialising in supply chain resilience, network science, and digital twin methods. My research develops quantitative frameworks to understand how supply chains fail under disruption — and how they can be made measurably more robust.

My work sits at the intersection of operations management, complex systems theory, and applied simulation, with a focus on real-world impact in semiconductor and electronics manufacturing supply chains. I am supervised by Prof. Dmitry Ivanov and serve as task leader in the ACCURATE Project (industrial partners: Airbus Atlantic, Continental, Tronico).
